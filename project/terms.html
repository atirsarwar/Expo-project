1. Acceptance of Terms

By accessing and using RAAS Campus Korangi's services, including but not limited to our website, admission portal, educational programs, and facilities, you agree to be bound by these Terms of Service and all applicable laws and regulations.

1.1 Eligibility

To use our services, you must:

  • Be at least 16 years of age
  • Provide accurate and complete information during registration
  • Maintain the security of your account credentials
  • Comply with all applicable laws and regulations

1.2 Account Responsibility

You are responsible for:

  • Maintaining the confidentiality of your account information
  • All activities that occur under your account
  • Immediately notifying us of any unauthorized use of your account

2. Admissions Policy

2.1 Admission Requirements

Admission to RAAS Campus Korangi programs requires:

  • Completion of minimum educational qualifications as specified for each program
  • Submission of all required documents
  • Successful completion of admission process including interview/assessment
  • Payment of applicable admission fees

2.2 Admission Decisions

Admission decisions are made based on:

  • Academic qualifications and performance
  • Interview/assessment results
  • Availability of seats in the program
  • Overall suitability for the chosen program

All admission decisions are final and at the sole discretion of the admission committee.

3. Fee Structure and Payment

3.1 Tuition Fees

All tuition fees are:

  • Payable in Pakistani Rupees (PKR)
  • Due as per the published fee schedule
  • Subject to revision with prior notice
  • Non-refundable except as specified in our refund policy

3.2 Payment Methods

We accept payments through:

  • Bank transfers/drafts
  • Cash payments at campus
  • Online payment gateways
  • Installment plans (where available)

3.3 Refund Policy

Refunds are processed under the following conditions:

  • Course cancellation by the institution: 100% refund
  • Withdrawal before course commencement: 75% refund
  • Withdrawal within first week: 50% refund
  • No refund after second week of course commencement

4. Student Code of Conduct

4.1 Expected Behavior

Students are expected to:

  • Maintain academic integrity and honesty
  • Respect faculty, staff, and fellow students
  • Attend classes regularly and punctually
  • Complete assignments and assessments on time
  • Follow campus rules and regulations

4.2 Prohibited Conduct

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:

  • Academic dishonesty including plagiarism and cheating
  • Harassment or discrimination of any kind
  • Damage to campus property
  • Unauthorized access to campus facilities
  • Use of illegal substances on campus
  • Violation of computer and network usage policies

4.3 Disciplinary Action

Violation of the code of conduct may result in:

  • Verbal or written warning
  • Suspension from classes
  • Expulsion from the program
  • Legal action where applicable

5. Academic Policies

5.1 Attendance Requirements

Students must maintain:

  • Minimum 75% attendance in each course
  • Regular participation in class activities
  • Completion of all required assignments and projects

5.2 Assessment and Grading

Academic performance is evaluated through:

  • Regular assignments and quizzes
  • Mid-term and final examinations
  • Practical projects and presentations
  • Class participation and attendance

5.3 Certification

Certificates are awarded to students who:

  • Successfully complete all course requirements
  • Maintain satisfactory academic standing
  • Clear all financial obligations
  • Meet attendance requirements

6. Privacy and Data Protection

6.1 Information Collection

We collect information necessary for:

  • Admission and enrollment processes
  • Academic record keeping
  • Communication with students and parents
  • Improvement of our services

6.2 Data Usage

Student data is used for:

  • Academic and administrative purposes
  • Communication regarding courses and events
  • Compliance with legal requirements
  • Institutional research and improvement

6.3 Data Protection

We implement appropriate security measures to protect student data, including:

  • Secure storage systems
  • Access controls and authentication
  • Regular security assessments
  • Staff training on data protection

7. Intellectual Property

7.1 Institutional IP

RAAS Campus Korangi retains all rights to:

  • Course materials and curriculum
  • Teaching methodologies and resources
  • Institutional branding and trademarks
  • Software and digital platforms

7.2 Student Work

Students retain rights to their original work, however:

  • The institution may use student work for promotional purposes with attribution
  • Group projects may involve shared intellectual property rights
  • Commercial use of institution-provided resources may require permission

8. Limitation of Liability

8.1 Educational Services

While we strive to provide quality education, we cannot guarantee:

  • Specific employment outcomes
  • Transferability of credits to other institutions
  • Individual student performance or success

8.2 Force Majeure

We are not liable for delays or failures in performance resulting from circumstances beyond our reasonable control, including:

  • Natural disasters
  • Government actions
  • Utility failures
  • Pandemics or health emergencies

9. Termination of Services

9.1 Student-Initiated Termination

Students may withdraw from programs by:

  • Submitting a formal withdrawal request
  • Clearing all outstanding dues
  • Returning institutional property

9.2 Institution-Initiated Termination

We may terminate services for:

  • Violation of terms and conditions
  • Non-payment of fees
  • Academic misconduct
  • Behavior detrimental to the institution

  • Modify these terms at any time
  • Update policies and procedures
  • Change course offerings and schedules

10.2 Notification

Significant changes will be communicated through:

  • Email notifications

  • Notice boards on campus
  • Direct communication with affected students
